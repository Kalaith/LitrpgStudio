# Writers Studio Roadmap

This roadmap focuses on the three outcomes that make Writers Studio *worth paying for*:

- a reliable canon vault (your source of truth),
- timeline confidence across scenes and books,
- and continuity “confidence checks” that speed up revision.

Everything else can come later.

## Guiding principles

- **Author-first:** readable, calm, and built for real writing habits.
- **Companion, not replacement:** works alongside Word/Scrivener/Docs.
- **Flexibility over templates:** support weird systems through custom entries and relationships.
- **Confidence over hype:** automation is optional and should reduce effort, not add anxiety.

---

## Milestone A: Canon Vault + Imports

**Goal:** Get writers into the product quickly, using their existing drafts, and give them a single reliable place for story facts.

**Key deliverables**
- Import draft content from common formats (e.g., Word exports, Scrivener compile exports, Markdown).
- Series → book → chapter/scene structure created from imported text.
- Canon vault for characters, locations, factions, items, rules, systems, and custom entry types.
- Custom fields (text/number/select) and relationships between entries (who knows what, where rules apply, what powers belong to whom).
- Fast search and “where was this mentioned?” backlinks to scenes.

**Exit criteria**
- A writer can import a draft and be productive in under 10 minutes.
- A story fact can be found in seconds, including where it was introduced.
- Custom lore types work well enough that most writers can model their “special system” without waiting on new features.

---

## Milestone B: Timeline Confidence

**Goal:** Prevent chronology mistakes before they spread.

**Key deliverables**
- Timeline event model connected to scenes/chapters/books.
- Views for order, gaps, overlaps, and “what happened when?”
- Conflict detection for impossible sequences and timing issues.

**Exit criteria**
- Timeline conflicts are surfaced automatically with clear context.
- Writers can resolve ordering issues without manual spreadsheeting.
- Major chronology contradictions are reduced in active drafts.

---

## Milestone C: Continuity Checks + Revision Workflow

**Goal:** Turn continuity checking into faster, calmer editing passes.

**Key deliverables**
- Confidence checks for common risks (character descriptions, relationships, world rules, timeline references).
- Clear explanations, severity levels, and links back to the relevant scenes.
- An issue list workflow: found → reviewed → fixed (with notes).
- Exportable reports/checklists for editors and beta readers.

**Exit criteria**
- Findings are understandable and actionable (low noise).
- Writers save real time during revision and feel more confident about consistency.
- The export/report output is useful in a real editing workflow.

---

## Later (backlog, only after A–C are solid)

These are valuable, but not required for the core “confidence companion” promise:

- A full drafting editor (if writers actually ask for it).
- Analytics and progress dashboards.
- Research/reference library and web clipping.
- A more conversational assistant and deeper personalization.
- Advanced multi-universe canon tooling (spin-offs, alternate timelines, etc.).

## Measurement (simple, not performative)

- Time to find a story fact.
- Number of continuity issues caught before beta readers.
- Time from “issue found” to “issue resolved”.
- Writer-reported confidence during revision.
